Audit of text-to-image engines · DALL-E × Imagen

Who does the machine imagine?

Hundreds of AI-generated portraits were produced from neutral occupational prompts — athlete, paramedic, neurosurgeon, tech CEO — then coded for apparent race and gender. The result is a synthetic population that looks almost nothing like the real one.

F.07Most diverse prompt

"Tech CEO" is the outlier — diverse by design?

The tech CEO prompt is the most diverse in the audit: a female majority (56.6%), strong Asian representation (23.6%), and almost no White generation (9.9%) — the inverse of the occupation's real-world profile, hinting at heavy-handed diversity correction on exactly one stereotype.

Method

Neutral occupational prompts were submitted to two text-to-image engines (DALL-E and Imagen). Outputs were coded for apparent race and gender, then aggregated per prompt and per engine. All figures are percentages of coded images; n is reported where the source run disclosed it (athlete n=227, paramedic n=271).

Caveats

"Apparent" race and gender are coder judgments of rendered images, not identities. U.S. population baselines are approximate. Categories marked "all other" are arithmetic remainders the source run did not itemize. Figures describe these engines, these prompts, this run — not all AI image generation.
